ABOUT ME

Menuka Ratnajeewa

Hi, I'm a Mechanical Engineering student at the University of Birmingham, originally based in Dubai. Cars and the machines that move people have been my way into engineering since before I knew the word "torque" — and that same fascination is still what drives me to pursue my dream of designing and building automotive systems - among other projects - that make a real difference for the environment, and the people who use them.

To me, one of the most important aspects of engineering is considering who's on the other end of the design. A faster gearbox is interesting. A gearbox that lets a specific person drive again and commute more safely - that's the project I actually want to design and develop.

This page collects that work: with real constraints and problems in mind, you will find my projects and designs, from university coursework to personal builds.

METHOD

How a build comes together

Find the person, not the problem

Every build starts by identifying someone with a recurring friction — a driver, a mechanic, a student — before any sketch happens. The constraint comes from them, not from what's fun to model.

Constrain it like an engineer

Cost, manufacturability, and the materials someone could actually source matter as much as performance. A solution nobody can build or afford isn't a solution.

Model, simulate, stress-test

CAD geometry, load cases, and tolerance checks come next — testing the idea against physics before it's tested against an audience.

Hand it back to the person it's for

The final measure of a build isn't how it looks rendered — it's whether the person it was designed for would actually want to use it.
